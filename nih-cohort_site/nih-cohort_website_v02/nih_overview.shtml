Mission

Electroencephalography (EEG) records and measures the electrical activity of the brain and the collected information are used to diagnose brain disorders and diseases, such as epilepsy and Alzheimer's disease. They are also used in research fields such as neuroscience, medicine, and bioengineering. However, EEG signals are complex signals and their interpretations vary by their observer which can lead to inconsistent diagnoses and slow down research progression.

Our proposed solution is automating the extraction of clinical information from a large database of EEG signals and EEG reports through a patient cohort retrieval system. This resource will allow users to search the large EEG archive and list patient records of EEGs relevant for interpretation of specific cases. It will help with diagnosing brain illnesses, conducting clinical research, and training young neurophysiologists. For proof-of-concept, this project uses the Temple University Hospital (TUH) EEG corpus which contains 25,000 sessions and 15,000 patients that have been collected over the span of 12 years, to create the patient cohort retrieval system.

The apparent challenges in this project are

  1. The EEG report formats from the the TUH EEG corpus vary between physicians.
  2. The interpretations and findings between physicians are inconsistent.
  3. Automatically processing EEG signals and reports.
  4. Searching patients based on their EEG reports and signals.

Aim 1: Automatically Recognize and Time-Align Events in EEG Signals

The EEG signal data will be automatically labeled based on 5 different events:

  • Spike and Sharp wave (SPK)
  • Generalized periodic epileptic discharges (GPED) and triaphasic
  • Periodic lateralized epileptiform discharges (PLED)
  • Eye blink (EBLY)
  • Artifact (ARTF)

Aim 2: Automatically Recognize Critical Clinical Concepts in the EEG Reports

We will automatically identify:

  • The patient's clinical history, conditions, and medication
  • The technique used to collect the EEG
  • Objective descriptions of the observeed activity, patterns, and events in the EEG

Aim 3: Automatic Patient Cohort Retrieval

Create a system that can accurately identify a desired patient population that will provide:

  • Annotated EEG reports and EEG signals with processed semantic information
  • The delivered EEG reports and signals search results will be ranked and listed in decreased order of relevance
  • Identification needed to specify patient cohorts when asked for a list of EEG reports
  • Organize EEG signal and report analysis using the qualified medical knowledge graph (QMKG) which will be used to increase the system's accuracy
<< Back || Next: Aim 4 >>

Aim 4: Evaluation and Analysis of the Results of the Patient Cohort Retrieval

  • Analyze the retrieved non-relevant EEG reports from a search request
  • Analyze relevant EEG reports that were not in the listed results
  • Improve the search requirements for clinical studies involving EEG data (e.g. "inclusion criteria" to describe the type of patients needed for a clinical study)
